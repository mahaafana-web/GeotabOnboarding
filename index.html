Set Up Your Accounts

These are the first things you need to do. Your ERP code and platform access are the foundation of your Geotab partnership.

Your ERP Code

Your ERP code is your internal Geotab account number. You will need it across all partner platforms and for every purchase, billing report, and support interaction. Keep it handy.

Provided in your welcome email

1

Create Your MyAdmin Account

What is MyAdmin? MyAdmin is your back-office platform — it's where you place hardware orders, manage device subscriptions, view billing and invoices, track shipments, submit RMA requests, and access support. Think of it as your operational hub for everything behind the scenes.

Visit myadmin.geotab.com and select "Create an account" to get started.

2

Notify Your Partner Account Manager

Once your MyAdmin account is created, notify your PAM so they can link your ERP code to your account. This step is required in order to access all of your Geotab systems and interact with Geotab.

3

Log In to PX Hub

What is PX Hub? The Partner Experience (PX) Hub is your partner-facing portal — it's where you register opportunities, access the Partner Learning Hub for training and certifications, download marketing and sales resources, and submit support requests. Think of it as your sales and enablement hub.

Single Sign-On: PX Hub now uses single sign-on with MyAdmin — no separate credentials needed. Your MyAdmin email and password are all you need.

To log in for the first time:

  1. Ask your Account Administrator to ensure your MyAdmin profile is Active and Approved with all General Info fields populated.
  2. Verify your correct ERP code is listed first under the Accounts tab in MyAdmin.
  3. Your Administrator assigns you a PX Hub role in MyAdmin (Executive, Manager, or General).
  4. Navigate to the PX Hub login page, select "Geotab Partner" as User Type, and sign in with your MyAdmin email and password.

Important: PX Hub currently supports only one account per user profile. It automatically uses the first account listed in your MyAdmin profile. If your organization’s Executive User is logging in for the first time, they will be prompted to accept the Geotab Partner Program Terms.

Login issues? Email pxhub@geotab.com  |  Full PX Hub Handbook

Go to PX Hub →
4

Add Your Team Members & Assign Roles

Your Account Administrator (the user with the UserManagement.Admin-Users role) manages all user access through MyAdmin under Configurations > User Management. To add a team member:

  1. Have the team member create their own MyAdmin account at myadmin.geotab.com.
  2. Ensure their profile is Active and Approved with all General Info fields populated and the correct ERP listed first under Accounts.
  3. Navigate to Configurations > User Management, find the user, and go to the Roles tab.
  4. Search "PXHub" and assign the appropriate role below.
Role Access Level
PXHub.Access-Executive Highest level — sees all users' data, can view partner scorecards and accept agreements. Only one Executive per organization.
PXHub.Access-Manager Mid level — sees their own data plus all General Users' data. Cannot see Executive or other Managers' data.
PXHub.Access-User Standard level — can only view and manage their own PX Hub data. Most common role for team members.
UserManagement.Admin-Users Account Administrator — can add/edit users and assign all roles in MyAdmin. Required for managing your team's access.

Each user should only be assigned one PX Hub role. If no one in your organization has the Account Administrator role yet, designate one person and ensure they have UserManagement.Admin-Users assigned first.

Pricing & Ordering

Everything you need to understand Geotab pricing, build your own customer pricing, and place your first order.

Your Customer, Your Contract

As a Geotab reseller, you own the customer relationship and the customer contract — not Geotab. This means:

  • You build your own customer terms and conditions — pricing, payment terms, service levels, and cancellation policies are yours to define
  • Pricing to your customers is at your own discretion and risk — Geotab provides partner pricing, but your retail rates and margins are your business decision
  • The customer contract is between you and your customer — Geotab is not a party to your customer agreements
  • Your customer agreements should reflect the subscription commitment — including the 36-month minimum term and any applicable early termination fees

Speak with your PAM if you need guidance on structuring your customer contracts.

1

Understand the Pricing Model

Geotab uses a bundle pricing model — hardware is included in the monthly subscription fee with no upfront device cost. Each subscription has a 36-month minimum term. If a customer terminates early, a prorated early termination fee (ETF) applies, equal to the remaining contract value.

Geotab offers several bundle plans ranging from basic telematics to full video safety solutions, with install bundle variants that include standard installation. Plans, pricing, and promo codes are all managed in MyAdmin — review the available options with your PAM to find the right fit for your customers.

Good to Know: Every bundle includes a GO device and a vehicle-specific wiring harness — the cable that connects the device to the vehicle's OBD-II port. There is no universal harness; every vehicle make, model, and year may require a different part number. Before placing any order, you must verify the correct harness for each vehicle using the Harness Lookup Tool.

Step 4

Device Installation

Get devices into vehicles and data flowing — this is where your customer starts seeing value.

Why Installation Matters

Installation is the moment your customer's investment becomes real. A smooth install builds trust and gets data flowing fast. A delayed or botched install means no data, no value, and a frustrated customer. Here's what's at stake:

  • Billing starts at activation — your subscription billing begins when a device is activated on a customer database, so a delayed install means delayed revenue for you
  • First impressions set the tone — a professional, on-time installation is often the customer's first hands-on experience with Geotab
  • Data quality depends on proper install — a correctly installed device delivers accurate GPS, engine diagnostics, and driver behavior data from day one
  • Poor installs drive churn — devices that aren't reporting or are producing bad data lead to support tickets, frustration, and cancellations

Choose Your Installation Path

You have three options — choose one or combine them based on your team size, geography, and customer needs.

1

Install In-House (Become a Certified Installer)

Best for: Resellers who want full control over scheduling, quality, and customer experience.

Complete the Geotab Installation Certification through Geotab Academy. Certification is required before performing any installations. Once certified, you can install GO devices and GO Focus cameras for your customers directly. This is the most common path for resellers with technicians on staff.

Typical install time: 15–30 minutes per vehicle for light-duty; 30–60 minutes for heavy-duty or vehicles requiring additional accessories.

Start Installation Certification →
2

Customer Self-Install (Provide Guides & Support)

Best for: Customers with their own fleet technicians or maintenance teams who prefer to handle installs internally.

Provide your customers with Geotab's vehicle-specific installation guides and the correct wiring harness for each vehicle. Many fleet customers — especially those with in-house mechanics — prefer to install devices themselves on their own schedule. Your role is to ensure they have the right harness, the right guide, and support if they need it.

Harness Lookup Tool → Vehicle Install Guides (by Make/Model) →
3

Use the Geotab Installation Network

Best for: Resellers without in-house technicians, or those needing national/multi-site coverage.

Leverage Geotab's network of certified third-party installers to handle installations on your behalf. This is ideal for large rollouts across multiple locations or when you need professional installation without building your own team. Geotab offers Install Bundle Plans that include installation in the subscription — pricing and availability are in MyAdmin.

Planning Your First Rollout

For your first customer deployment, plan the rollout in manageable batches rather than all at once:

  • Start with a pilot batch — install 5–10 vehicles first, verify data is flowing correctly in MyGeotab, and resolve any issues before scaling
  • Verify the correct harness before ordering — use the Harness Lookup Tool for every VIN in the fleet (mixed fleets often require multiple harness types)
  • Coordinate with the fleet's schedule — installations require vehicle downtime, so work with the customer to schedule around their operations
  • Have spare harnesses on hand — order a few extras in case of damaged connectors or last-minute vehicle additions

Harness Selection & Verification

As covered in the Pricing section, every bundle includes a vehicle-specific wiring harness. Getting the right one is critical to a successful install. Here are the key considerations:

  • Mixed fleets need multiple harness types — a fleet with Ford F-150s and Kenworth T680s will require completely different harnesses for each
  • Heavy-duty vehicles (Mack, Kenworth, Peterbilt, Freightliner) often use 6-pin or 9-pin diagnostic connectors instead of standard OBD-II, requiring specialized harnesses
  • Always verify by VIN — enter each vehicle's VIN in the Harness Lookup Tool to get the exact part number and install guide before ordering
  • No result? If a VIN does not return a match, contact your PAM or Geotab support before placing the order
Harness Lookup Tool → Vehicle Install Guides →

Electric Vehicle (EV) Installations

Electric vehicles have unique installation requirements. EV harnesses differ from ICE (internal combustion engine) vehicles, and some EVs require specific mounting locations or additional configuration in MyGeotab to report battery state-of-charge and charging data correctly. Always verify EV compatibility using the Harness Lookup Tool and consult the vehicle-specific install guide. If the EV model is not listed, contact your PAM before ordering.

After Installation: Verify & Activate

Every install is only complete once the device is reporting data. After installing a GO device:

  1. Turn the vehicle ignition on and wait 2–3 minutes for the device to power up and establish a GPS fix
  2. Log in to MyGeotab and confirm the device appears on the live map with a current location
  3. Take a short test drive — verify that trip data, speed, and engine metrics are recording correctly
  4. Check for fault codes or warnings — if the device shows communication errors, verify the harness connection and seating
  5. Confirm with the customer — walk them through the MyGeotab dashboard so they can see their data is live

Subscription billing begins when the device is activated on a customer database. Devices that are ordered but not installed do not incur subscription charges.
